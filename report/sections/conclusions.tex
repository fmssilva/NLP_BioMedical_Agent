\subsection{Achievements}
\label{sec:achievements}

\paragraph{Phase 1 --- Retrieval:}
We delivered a complete, modular IR pipeline covering corpus loading,
indexing, query building, retrieval, evaluation, and result serialisation ---
all Colab-compatible.%
\footnote{Phase~1 notebook:
\url{https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1UvDhFXYEzZxse6gknDyBSUaq0EAHZkFV?usp=sharing}.}
Five retrieval strategies (BM25, LM-JM, LM-Dirichlet, dense KNN, RRF)
were implemented and compared following a strict tuning protocol with the
test set touched exactly once.
The most impactful design choice was selecting MedCPT
($\Delta$NDCG@100=+0.0501 on test, +0.0825 on CV versus the msmarco
baseline).
The locked Phase~2 backbone, KNN\,(MedCPT), achieves NDCG@100=0.8077,
MAP=0.6143, and R@100=0.8933 on 33 test topics; RRF (BM25+MedCPT)
pushes test NDCG@100 to 0.8245.
The R@100 of 0.8933 ensures over 89\% of relevant documents are available
for Phase~2 generation.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Phase 1 --- Retrieval:}
Relevance judgements are citation-based from automated BioGen submissions,
not human assessors, so systematic biases are possible.
The mean of 46.1 relevant documents per topic
($3\text{--}15\times$ above standard TREC tasks) inflates MAP and recall
figures.
The 33-topic test set is small, the fixed odd/even split offers no guarantee
of balanced topic difficulty, and a handful of qrel PMIDs are absent from
the corpus, creating an irreducible recall ceiling.
Finally, production KNN retrieval relies on HNSW indexing, which introduces
some recall loss compared to the brute-force cosine used during encoder
selection.
